Field Force Management Platform

A mobile-first platform that brings distributed field teams — sales agents, delivery staff, on-ground operations — onto one dashboard, replacing paper-based visit plans and untracked attendance with real-time visibility.

Overview

What it does

This Field Force Management platform is built for businesses that run distributed sales teams and field agents. It lets companies track their workforce in real time, plan and manage field visits, handle orders and payments, and streamline day-to-day field operations from a single unified dashboard — bridging office management with what's actually happening on the ground.

The problem

Why it needed to exist

Managing a distributed field workforce is chaotic by default: attendance goes untracked, visit plans exist only on paper, orders get logged manually, and managers have no visibility into what agents are actually doing during the day. This platform replaces those fragmented processes with a centralized, mobile-first system — giving managers full control and giving field agents one simple tool to log their entire workday.

My role

What I designed

End-to-end product design — information architecture, the sidebar navigation structure that organizes all 19 modules, module-level screen design, and interactive prototyping in Figma.

Design highlights

How it holds together

  • Clean, sidebar-first navigation with a consistent orange-and-white design language applied across all 19 modules.
  • Every module follows the same repeatable pattern — list view → detail view → create/edit form → reports — so the interface stays predictable as it grows.
  • Kept the learning curve low for field agents who may not be highly tech-savvy, prioritizing familiarity over novelty.
  • Every module includes its own Settings and Reports & Analytics submodule, reflecting enterprise-grade information architecture.
