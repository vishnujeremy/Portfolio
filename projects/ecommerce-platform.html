Overview

What it does

This is a comprehensive e-commerce ecosystem that lets businesses manage products, inventory, procurement, warehousing, order fulfillment, customer relationships, payments, logistics, and retail POS operations from a single centralized platform. It was built to simplify daily operations by connecting every stage of the commerce lifecycle — from supplier procurement and inventory management to customer orders, shipping, returns, and reporting — while giving administrators complete visibility and control.

A major focus throughout was reducing operational complexity through intuitive interfaces, clear information architecture, workflow automation, and a design system built to scale alongside a growing business.

My role

What I designed

I designed the complete user experience and interface for both the customer-facing website and the admin dashboard — user flow planning, information architecture, wireframing, high-fidelity UI, component creation, responsive layouts, dashboard optimization, and reusable design patterns to keep the platform consistent as it grew.

Design process

How I got there

  • Conducted requirement analysis with business stakeholders before drawing a single screen.
  • Created information architecture for a genuinely complex set of interconnected business workflows.
  • Designed end-to-end user journeys, then built wireframes and interactive prototypes on top of them.
  • Built reusable UI components and design system elements to keep the platform consistent as it grew.
  • Optimized workflows specifically to minimize clicks and improve operational efficiency.
  • Worked closely with developers throughout to keep designs feasible and implementation-consistent.
Challenges solved

What made this hard

The core challenge was simplifying highly complex business operations into intuitive workflows, and reducing navigation complexity across dozens of interconnected modules. The architecture needed to support future business growth without a redesign, while dashboard-driven analytics and reporting improved operational visibility. Reusable components kept the design consistent across the application, and enterprise-level functionality had to stay balanced against a clean, approachable interface that didn't demand a steep learning curve from administrators.
